Pokémon Ability Guide

This document describes Pokémon abilities, which are passive effects that influence battle mechanics. Each Pokémon can have one or more abilities that activate automatically under certain conditions.

Abilities can affect damage, status conditions, weather, switching mechanics, and other battle interactions.

--------------------------------------------------
What is an Ability?
--------------------------------------------------
An ability is a passive trait assigned to a Pokémon that provides special effects in battle or in the overworld. Abilities do not require activation by the player and are always in effect unless suppressed.

Each Pokémon species may have:
- One or two standard abilities
- One hidden ability (rarer, often obtained through special methods)

--------------------------------------------------
Ability Categories
--------------------------------------------------

1. Offensive Abilities
These abilities increase damage output or apply pressure to opponents.

Examples:
- Huge Power: Doubles the Pokémon’s Attack stat
- Blaze: Boosts Fire-type moves when HP is low
- Adaptability: Increases same-type attack bonus (STAB)

--------------------------------------------------

2. Defensive Abilities
These abilities reduce damage taken or provide immunity.

Examples:
- Levitate: Grants immunity to Ground-type moves
- Thick Fat: Reduces damage from Fire and Ice moves
- Sturdy: Prevents being knocked out in one hit from full HP

--------------------------------------------------

3. Status-Based Abilities
These abilities interact with status conditions.

Examples:
- Guts: Increases Attack when affected by a status condition
- Immunity: Prevents poisoning
- Synchronize: Passes status conditions to the opponent

--------------------------------------------------

4. Weather-Based Abilities
These abilities activate or benefit from weather conditions.

Examples:
- Drizzle: Summons rain when entering battle
- Drought: Summons harsh sunlight
- Swift Swim: Doubles Speed in rain

--------------------------------------------------

5. Utility Abilities
These provide unique effects that influence gameplay.

Examples:
- Intimidate: Lowers opponent’s Attack upon entry
- Pressure: Causes opponent to use more PP
- Pickup: May obtain items after battle

--------------------------------------------------
Ability Mechanics
--------------------------------------------------

- Abilities activate automatically without player input
- Some abilities trigger on entry into battle
- Some abilities activate under specific conditions (e.g., low HP, weather)
- Certain moves and abilities can suppress or ignore abilities

--------------------------------------------------
Hidden Abilities
--------------------------------------------------

Hidden abilities are special abilities that are less common and often provide competitive advantages.

Examples:
- Protean: Changes the Pokémon’s type to match its move
- Magic Bounce: Reflects status moves back to the opponent
