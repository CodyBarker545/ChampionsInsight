Weather Effects Guide

Weather conditions are battlefield effects that influence damage, abilities, and other battle mechanics. Weather typically lasts for several turns and can be extended by certain abilities or items.

--------------------------------------------------
Types of Weather
--------------------------------------------------

Rain:
- Boosts Water-type moves by 50%
- Reduces Fire-type move damage by 50%
- Activates certain abilities (e.g., Swift Swim, Rain Dish)

Sun (Harsh Sunlight):
- Boosts Fire-type moves by 50%
- Reduces Water-type move damage by 50%
- Activates certain abilities (e.g., Chlorophyll, Solar Power)

Sandstorm:
- Damages most Pokémon each turn (1/16 of max HP)
- Does not damage Rock, Ground, or Steel types
- Boosts Special Defense of Rock-type Pokémon by 50%
- Activates abilities such as Sand Rush and Sand Force

Snow (Hail):
- Damages most Pokémon each turn (1/16 of max HP)
- Does not damage Ice-type Pokémon
- May provide defensive boosts to Ice-types (depending on generation)
- Activates abilities such as Slush Rush

--------------------------------------------------
Weather Mechanics
--------------------------------------------------

- Weather conditions typically last 5 turns
- Duration can be extended to 8 turns with held items (e.g., Damp Rock, Heat Rock)
- Weather can be changed by moves or abilities
- Only one weather condition can be active at a time

--------------------------------------------------
Ability Interaction
--------------------------------------------------

Many abilities depend on weather conditions to activate or gain benefits.

Examples:
- Drizzle: Automatically sets rain
- Drought: Automatically sets sunlight
- Swift Swim: Doubles Speed in rain
- Chlorophyll: Doubles Speed in sunlight
