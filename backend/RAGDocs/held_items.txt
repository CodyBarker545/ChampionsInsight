Held Items Guide (Complete Categories)

Held items are items a Pokémon can hold to gain passive effects in battle. These effects can increase stats, restore HP, modify moves, or trigger under specific conditions.

Held items are divided into categories based on their effects.

--------------------------------------------------
1. Berries (Consumable Items)
--------------------------------------------------

Berries are one-time use items that activate automatically.

Types of Berries:

HP Recovery Berries:
- Restore HP when below a threshold (e.g., Sitrus Berry restores ~25% HP)

Status-Healing Berries:
- Cure status conditions (e.g., Lum Berry cures any status)

Damage-Reducing Berries:
- Reduce damage from a specific type (e.g., Occa Berry reduces Fire damage)

Stat-Boosting Berries:
- Increase a stat when HP is low (e.g., Salac Berry boosts Speed)

Berries are consumed after activation.

--------------------------------------------------
2. Choice Items
--------------------------------------------------

Choice items increase a stat but restrict move usage.

Examples:
- Choice Band: +50% Attack
- Choice Specs: +50% Special Attack
- Choice Scarf: +50% Speed

Effect:
- User is locked into one move until switching out

--------------------------------------------------
3. Type-Enhancing Items
--------------------------------------------------

These items boost moves of a specific type.

Effect:
- Increase damage of matching type moves by ~20%

Examples:
- Charcoal (Fire)
- Mystic Water (Water)
- Magnet (Electric)

--------------------------------------------------
4. Recovery Items
--------------------------------------------------

These provide healing over time or under conditions.

Examples:
- Leftovers: Restores 1/16 HP each turn
- Black Sludge: Heals Poison-types, damages others

--------------------------------------------------
5. Damage-Boosting Items
--------------------------------------------------

Increase damage output with trade-offs.

Examples:
- Life Orb: +30% damage, loses 10% HP per attack
- Expert Belt: Boosts super-effective moves

--------------------------------------------------
6. Defensive Items
--------------------------------------------------

Reduce damage or improve survivability.

Examples:
- Assault Vest: +50% Special Defense, cannot use status moves
- Eviolite: Boosts Defense and Special Defense if the Pokémon can evolve

--------------------------------------------------
7. Trigger-Based Items
--------------------------------------------------

Activate under certain conditions.

Examples:
- Focus Sash: Survive with 1 HP from full health
- Weakness Policy: Boosts Attack and Special Attack when hit by super-effective move

--------------------------------------------------
8. Utility Items
--------------------------------------------------

Provide unique effects not directly tied to damage.

Examples:
- Rocky Helmet: Damages attackers on contact
- Air Balloon: Grants temporary Ground immunity
- Heavy-Duty Boots: Prevents hazard damage

--------------------------------------------------
9. Form-Changing Items
--------------------------------------------------

Change a Pokémon’s form or typing.

Examples:
- Plates (Arceus type change)
- Drives (Genesect move type)
- Memories (Silvally type change)

--------------------------------------------------
10. Special Battle Mechanics Items
--------------------------------------------------

Items tied to special mechanics.

Examples:
- Mega Stones: Enable Mega Evolution
- Z-Crystals: Enable Z-Moves
- Gems: Boost a move once

--------------------------------------------------
Item Mechanics
--------------------------------------------------

- A Pokémon can hold only one item at a time
- Some items are consumed after activation
- Items can be removed or swapped
- Many items activate automatically without input
