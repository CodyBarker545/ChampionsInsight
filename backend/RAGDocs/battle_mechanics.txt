Battle Mechanics Guide

This document describes core mechanics that determine how damage and turn order work in Pokémon battles.

--------------------------------------------------
Same-Type Attack Bonus (STAB)
--------------------------------------------------

- If a Pokémon uses a move that matches its type, the move’s damage is increased
- Standard STAB multiplier: 1.5×
- Some abilities (e.g., Adaptability) increase STAB to 2×

--------------------------------------------------
Critical Hits
--------------------------------------------------

- Critical hits deal increased damage (1.5×)
- Ignore negative stat changes on the attacker
- Ignore positive stat boosts on the defender
- Occur randomly, with increased chance for certain moves and abilities

--------------------------------------------------
Turn Order and Speed
--------------------------------------------------

- Pokémon act in order of Speed stat (higher Speed moves first)
- Priority moves can override Speed (e.g., Quick Attack)
- Status conditions like Paralysis reduce Speed and affect turn order

--------------------------------------------------
Accuracy and Evasion
--------------------------------------------------

- Moves have an accuracy percentage that determines if they hit
- Accuracy and evasion can be modified by moves and abilities
- Some moves never miss under certain conditions

--------------------------------------------------
Damage Basics
--------------------------------------------------

Damage depends on:
- Attacking stat (Attack or Special Attack)
- Defending stat (Defense or Special Defense)
- Move power
- Type effectiveness
- STAB bonus
- Random variation

--------------------------------------------------
Key Rules
--------------------------------------------------

- Type effectiveness and STAB stack together
- Critical hits are calculated after most modifiers
- Only one Pokémon acts at a time per turn
- Battle outcomes depend on combining multiple mechanics
