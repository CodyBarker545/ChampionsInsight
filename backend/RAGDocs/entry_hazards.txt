Entry Hazards Guide

Entry hazards are battlefield effects that damage or affect Pokémon when they switch into battle. 
They remain on the field until removed and primarily impact grounded Pokémon.

--------------------------------------------------
Types of Entry Hazards
--------------------------------------------------

Stealth Rock:
- Deals damage based on the Pokémon’s weakness or resistance to Rock-type moves
- Damage ranges from 1/32 (resisted) up to 1/2 (4× weakness) of max HP

Spikes:
- Damages grounded Pokémon when they switch in
- 1 layer: 1/8 of max HP
- 2 layers: 1/6 of max HP
- 3 layers: 1/4 of max HP

Toxic Spikes:
- Poisons grounded Pokémon when they switch in
- 1 layer: regular poison
- 2 layers: badly poisoned (increasing damage each turn)

Sticky Web:
- Lowers Speed of grounded Pokémon upon switch-in

--------------------------------------------------
Hazard Mechanics
--------------------------------------------------

- Only affect Pokémon when they enter the field
- Flying-type Pokémon and those with Levitate are immune to ground-based hazards (Spikes, Toxic Spikes, Sticky Web)
- Stealth Rock affects all Pokémon regardless of grounding
- Multiple hazards can be active at the same time
- Hazards can be removed by moves such as Rapid Spin or Defog

--------------------------------------------------
Competitive Impact
--------------------------------------------------

- Hazards apply constant pressure by dealing damage over time
- They punish frequent switching
- They can break Focus Sash and reduce survivability
- Often used in competitive play to control the pace of battle

--------------------------------------------------
Ability Interaction
--------------------------------------------------

Some abilities interact with entry hazards.

Examples:
- Magic Guard: Prevents damage from hazards
- Levitate: Grants immunity to ground-based hazards
- Overcoat: Prevents damage from certain indirect effects
