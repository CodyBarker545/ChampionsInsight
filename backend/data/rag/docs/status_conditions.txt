Pokémon Status Conditions Guide

This document describes status conditions, which are effects that impact a Pokémon’s ability to battle. These conditions can reduce stats, prevent actions, or deal damage over time.

Status conditions are divided into two main categories: non-volatile and volatile.

--------------------------------------------------
Types of Status Conditions
--------------------------------------------------

1. Non-Volatile Status Conditions
These are major status conditions that persist even after switching out and often last until healed.

A Pokémon can only have ONE non-volatile status condition at a time.

Examples include:
- Burn
- Paralysis
- Poison
- Sleep
- Freeze

These conditions usually require healing through items, abilities, or visiting a Pokémon Center.

--------------------------------------------------

2. Volatile Status Conditions
These are temporary effects that only last during battle or for a limited number of turns.

A Pokémon can have multiple volatile conditions at the same time.

Examples include:
- Confusion
- Flinch
- Infatuation

These conditions typically wear off after a few turns or when the Pokémon switches out.

--------------------------------------------------
Common Status Conditions
--------------------------------------------------

Burn:
- Deals damage each turn (1/16 of max HP)
- Reduces physical Attack stat by 50%

Paralysis:
- Reduces Speed to 50% of its original value
- 25% chance each turn that the Pokémon cannot move

Poison:
- Deals damage each turn (1/8 of max HP)

Badly Poisoned (Toxic):
- Damage increases each turn (starts at 1/16, then increases each turn)

Sleep:
- Prevents the Pokémon from acting for 1–3 turns

Freeze:
- Prevents the Pokémon from acting
- Has a chance each turn to thaw (or can be cured by certain moves)

Confusion (volatile):
- Lasts 1–4 turns
- Each turn, there is a chance (~33%) the Pokémon hurts itself instead of attacking

--------------------------------------------------
Battle Impact
--------------------------------------------------

- Status conditions can significantly weaken a Pokémon
- Some abilities and moves rely on status conditions
- Certain types are immune to specific conditions
  (e.g., Steel-types cannot be poisoned)

--------------------------------------------------
Healing and Removal
--------------------------------------------------

Status conditions can be removed by:
- Items (e.g., Full Heal)
- Moves (e.g., Aromatherapy)
- Abilities (e.g., Natural Cure)
- Visiting a Pokémon Center
